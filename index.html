Project Overview

This project explores how commissioned inpatient mental health services meet the needs of women in the perinatal period. It focuses on understanding access to Mother and Baby Units (MBUs) and the realities of care on acute and psychiatric intensive care (PICU) wards.

Background & Rationale

Across Yorkshire and Humber, women in the perinatal period may be admitted to local MBUs, out-of-area MBUs, or non-MBU wards. The system currently lacks a clear, evidence-based understanding of how pathways operate for women not admitted to MBUs.

Quality Oversight

Through in-depth case manager reviews, clinical record analysis, and stakeholder engagement, we monitor quality and map referral routes, decision points, and care pathways.

About the Project

This six-week scoping project explores how inpatient mental health care is accessed and experienced by women in the perinatal period admitted to non-MBU wards in Leeds.

Key Aims & Objectives

  • Understand referral pathways, including MBU vs non-MBU bed use
  • Build insight into decisions around admissions, care planning, and delivery
  • Explore how step downs from non-MBU to MBU wards happen in practice
  • Support sustainable commissioning and improve access to MBUs

Core Principles

  • Trauma-informed, collaborative, appreciative enquiry
  • Focus on women's, babies', and families' experiences
  • Co-production with service users and staff

Project Timeline

Project commences 29th September 2025

Week 1-2: Stakeholder Engagement

Engagement with ward staff, liaison teams, and care coordinators

Week 2-4: Case Studies

Case studies of nine women referred to MBU but admitted to acute/PICU wards in 2023-24

Week 3-5: Clinical Review

Clinical record review to provide contextual insights

Week 4-6: Analysis

Thematic analysis of staff and women's feedback

Week 6: Synthesis

Synthesis and recommendations; report shared with PC and Assurance Board

Stakeholder Engagement

Who's Involved

  • LYPFT acute and PICU wards
  • Community perinatal mental health team, liaison services
  • Care coordinators, peer support workers
  • Commissioning Hub, ICBs, RMS

Patient & Family Voice

We involve women, babies, and families through case studies using an appreciative enquiry approach. Women are invited to share their experiences in a constructive, respectful way, supported by care coordinators or peer support workers.

Key Research Questions

Pathway Structure

How is the inpatient pathway for perinatal women structured and delivered in West Yorkshire?

Experience Assessment

What are the experiences of women and clinicians when accessing and delivering care?

Complex Needs

How are the needs of women with complex/high-risk presentations met when not admitted to MBUs?

Step Down Process

How are step downs to MBUs considered and managed?
